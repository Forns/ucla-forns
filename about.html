About

Undergraduate

As a southern-Californian, born and raised, it's been pretty tough to leave paradise behind for education and employment alike... So I decided not to! My undergraduate career took place at Loyola Marymount University (just a few miles south of UCLA) where I studied computer science, psychology, and a little pure mathematics for good measure. During my tenure at LMU, I developed a passion for studying artificial agents, their interaction with humans, and the formal mechanisms that drive them. My theses at LMU concerted elements of cognitive psychology with artificial intelligence; I continue research along this vein with professors at LMU to this day.

Related to the study of artificial agents, I am also a board member for the Immersive Education Initiative (iED West Coast Chapter), where I serve to organize conferences for, and examine the leading technologies in, immersive electronic devices and applications for improving education. I became involved with the organization at LMU through collaboration with the iED West Chair, Dr. Richard Gilbert.

Graduate

If my work in undergrad produced the "What" of my interests in artificial agents, then my graduate work is a study of the "How." At UCLA, I'm a member of the Cognitive Systems Laboratory under the advisement of Dr. Judea Pearl, studying a field in artificial intelligence known as Causal Reasoning. In particular, I'm examining how machines learn in terms of cause-and-effect, and how quantifying this cognitive task can lead to advances in reasoning systems. Formally and currently, I study causal analyses in sequential decision problems: how machines learn over time.

Future Directions

I hope to continue research in cognitive systems and apply causal theory to interactive, intelligent agents. I also truly enjoy teaching and would thoroughly enjoy a professorship once my graduate education is complete. Look for me in the future; short of a professorship, Forney Industries might soon be a household name...
